title: Alignment is the constraint, not a hypothesis to measure
---

The dissent challenged: "measure agent-scoping-accuracy before building more." But alignment isn't a hypothesis — it's a constraint. You don't measure whether gravity applies before building a bridge.

Session 1 IS the evidence: the agent used oh_get_outcomes before making decisions, recorded metis after learning, used outcome_progress to check progress, created AGENTS.md from MCP tool queries, tagged commits with [outcome:X]. That's not a measurement — it's the system working.

The "validate-before-building" guardrail was right about not adding embeddings/LSP prematurely. But it was wrong to frame the core alignment thesis as something that needs A/B testing. Act on strong beliefs. Measure what you're uncertain about.
